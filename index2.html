Problem of Unique Identification

Kaan Volkan
June 2025

Goal: Decouple the pulsing and traveling components of a wave representing chemical concentrations.

Proposition: It is impossible to separately identify the pulsing and traveling.

Key Definitions

  • Traveling wave: The observed phenomena can be modeled through the spatial translation of pre-existing RT.
  • Pulse: The observed phenomena can be modeled as new RTs that come into existence or are destroyed.

The Discrete Model

This model allows us to study a simplified version. It is inspired by the toy model representing the “Fermionic System with Evenly Spaced Levels” from An Introduction to Thermal Physics by Daniel V. Schroeder.

The model is 1 dimensional with infinite domain reaching into negative and positive infinity.

The dots above the line segments represent the RT concentration.

The dots above the line segments represent the RD concentration.

Result

You can see that the end results of both processes are the exact same if you only look at the RT concentration, but the processes are completely different. This situation only gets more complicated in 2 dimensions, and when the two motions are combined.

What does this simple observation tell us? You can only differentiate moving and pulsing motion by observing the concentrations of RT and RD simultaneously.
